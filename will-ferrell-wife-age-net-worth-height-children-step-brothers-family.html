Will Ferrell Wife, Age, Net worth, Height, Children, Step Brothers, Family

Will Ferrell’s rise to stardom is filled with stories as funny as the roles he plays. As an American actor, comedian, producer and writer, Ferrell has carved a sacred place in American cinematography and in the hearts of Americans. Beyond his acting career, however, Will has a life that is every bit as interesting as the character he plays on screen. Here you can find out all about his background, family life and finances.

biography, age

Born July 16, 1967 in Irvine, California. John William “Will” Ferrell’s ancestry includes English, German and Irish. He is the older of two siblings whose parents went their separate ways and later divorced when he was only 8 years old. Though the divorce was amicable and both parents were committed to the well-being of their children, Ferrell never forgets the financial struggle his father faced in order to fulfill his side of the deal. His paychecks were as fickle as show business at the time, and he could be months away from his family at times. These informed Ferrell’s decision to pursue a more steady stream of income later in his adult years. a decision fate would not allow him to keep.

Will Ferrell’s fun demeanor and penchant for comedy (during high school) were the first signs that apples weren’t far off the pedigree after all. Ferrell would continue to follow in his father’s footsteps, albeit more successfully.

While attending high school, Ferrell secured a job at an auction house with the help of his friend Viveca Paulin. The work allowed him a degree of freedom; go to auditions and rehearsals while working on the side. He once served as Santa Claus for a mall.

After graduating high school, Ferrell enrolled at the University of Southern California (USC), where he received his bachelor’s degree in sports information in 1990. Despite being the right person for the industry, Ferrell remained reluctant to pursue a career in show business, opting instead for a Steady 9-5 job. However, he soon succumbed to fate when his multiple attempts to find steady employment proved abortive.

In 1991 he finally moved to Los Angeles, California to pursue a career in show business. He began performing stand-up comedy skits, which at the time proved unsuccessful. Ferrell soon expanded his attempts into other genres of comedy; be satisfied later with improvisation. He also excelled in comedy roles that involved impersonations. His favorite character is Harry Caray, the Hall of Fame baseball announcer. Ferrell soon began creating original characters with teammate Chris Kattan. In 1994 he successfully auditioned and won an appearance in the popular Los Angeles comedy group The Groundlings.

Around the turn of 1995, New York-based comedy show, Saturday Night Live (SNL) began to experience a steady decline in popularity. Lorne Michaels, desperate to save the show, auditioned for Ferrell and two other colleagues. Chris Kattan and Cheri Oteri. After a remarkable audition performance, Will Ferrell joined SNL and served for the next seven years; Ferrell has since returned to be a guest host on SNL in May 2005; May 2009; May 2012; and January 2018.

Ferrell has done well as a cast member for SNL, with notable impersonations including former US President George W. Bush, Vice President Al Gore, US Senator Ted Kennedy, singers Robert Goulet and Neil Diamond, Iraqi President Saddam Hussein and the Cuban President Fidel Castro among others. In a 2014 poll, Ferrell was voted the best SNL cast member of all time.

Although Ferrell has appeared in various films, his first starring role after leaving SNL was Frank “The Tank” Richard in the 2003 film Old School . Old School became an overnight sensation and Ferrell received an MTV Movie Awards nomination for Best Comedic Performance. The New York Times described how Ferrell’s hilarious, timid zeal sold Old School to millions of viewers. Some of his other film work includes Zoolander (2001), Eleven (2003), Anchorman (2004), Blades of Glory (2007), Step Brothers (2008), Grow Strong (2015) and Zoolander II (2016).

salary and wealth

A Successful Career On SNL, Will Ferrell rose to become the highest-paid performer in 2001 with a salary of over $350,000. After leaving SNL, his film success continued with top theatrical releases such as Elf (2003), Anchorman: The Legend of Ron Burgundy (2004). Melinda and Melinda (2004), Talladega Nights: The Ballad of Ricky Bobby (2006), and others. In 2005, Ferrell’s total earnings were estimated at $40 million.

Things reached a turning point for the Irvine-born actor after he grossed over $7 million for his role in the 2004 film Anchorman: The Legend of Ron Burgundy . Soon after, Ferrell’s film career began churn of $20 million per role paychecks. And in 2009, he became a common name on the Forbes list of overpaid Hollywood actors. This list shows well-known actors and actresses who failed to make the films (in which they starred) a combined box-office hit, despite being well paid for their roles. Will Ferrell’s net worth is currently $100 million.

Will Ferrell’s family – wife, children, stepbrothers

While the world only knows one funny and weird actor, there’s more to Will Ferrell than what we see on screen. He has a close family with his lovely wife Viveca Paulin and three adorable boys – Magnus, Mattias and Axel. All three appeared in Anchorman 2. Aside from his stepbrother Dale in the film, Stepbrothers, Will Ferrell does not have a stepbrother in real life.

Height

Will Ferrell is 6 feet 3 inches or 1.95 m tall and weighs 90 kg. He has blue-green eyes and hair that has been described as salt and pepper.
