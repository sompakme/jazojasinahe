E. Jean Carroll (born December 12, 1943) is a writer and advice columnist based in the United States. Originally published in Elle magazine in 1993, her “Ask E. Jean” column has subsequently been named one of the top five magazine articles in the country (together with Anthony Lane of The New Yorker and Lewis Lapham of Harper’s Magazine) by the Chicago Tribune in 200She was born Elizabeth Jean Carroll in Detroit, Michigan, and was given the nickname “Betty Jean” from an early age. Carroll’s views on sex, her impatient, boisterous advice that women should “never never” wrap their lives around men, and her compassion for letter-writers who are suffering through life’s trials and tribulations distinguish her column from others in the women’s magazine industry. ‘Ask E. Jean’ launch was a memorable experience for Amy Gross, former editor-in-chief of Elle and current editor-in-chief of O, The Oprah Magazine, who remembers it fondly. As one observer described it, “it was as if we had saddled her up on a bucking bronco and her responses were the shouts, whoops, and hollers of an exuberant lady having a good ol’ time.” From 1994 to 1996, NBC’s cable channel, America’s Talking, broadcast the Ask E. Jean television programme, which was based on the syndicated column (when the channel became MSNBC). Carroll has been dubbed “[t]he most entertaining cable chat show host you will ever watch” by the magazine Entertainment Weekly. In his review of E. Jean for TV Guide, Jeff Jarvis said that the show’s “robotic hyperactivity drove [him] completely insane.” “However, after that, I listened to her and couldn’t help but like her.” he said. “E. Jean provides sound advise. ” For her writing for Saturday Night Live (1985), Carroll was nominated for an Emmy, and for her work on the Ask E. Jean television show, she was nominated for a Cable Ace Award (1995). The AskEJean.com website, which is based on the Elle column, is a live experiment in the compelling ways in which people give and receive advice that is still ongoing. Questions about career, beauty, sex, men, diet, “sticky situations,” and friends can be typed in, and users will receive instant video responses. Alternatively, users can become a member of the Advice Vixens (a section in which “YOU become the advice columnist”) “Top Campus Sex Columnists” is a collection of the best college advice columnists from across the United States of America.
