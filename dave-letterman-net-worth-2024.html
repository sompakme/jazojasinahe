Early Career and Breakthrough

Letterman’s career began in the 1970s as a radio talk show host and weatherman in Indiana. His unique style and humor caught the attention of talent scouts, leading to appearances on shows like “The Tonight Show Starring Johnny Carson.” These appearances set the stage for his own morning comedy show, “The David Letterman Show,” which, despite its short run, earned him critical acclaim and two Emmy Awards.

The Rise of Late Night Stardom

In 1982, Letterman became the host of “Late Night with David Letterman” on NBC, which quickly became a hit with audiences. His offbeat humor and innovative segments redefined the late-night talk show format. The success of “Late Night” significantly boosted Letterman’s profile and earnings.

Transition to CBS and Continued Success

After a highly publicized battle for “The Tonight Show” following Johnny Carson’s retirement, Letterman moved to CBS in 1993 to host “The Late Show with David Letterman.” The move included a lucrative deal that not only increased his salary but also gave him ownership of the show through his production company, Worldwide Pants Incorporated.

Worldwide Pants Incorporated

Letterman’s production company, Worldwide Pants, was a significant contributor to his wealth. It produced successful shows like “Everybody Loves Raymond” and “The Late Late Show with Craig Ferguson,” which added to Letterman’s earnings through syndication and licensing deals.

Endorsements and Appearances

While not known for traditional product endorsements, Letterman’s brand and influence led to various lucrative appearances and guest spots on other shows and events. These opportunities provided additional income streams beyond his television work.

Real Estate Investments

He owns several properties across the United States, including a sprawling ranch in Montana, a residence in St. Barth’s, and a home in New York. Car Collection

Retirement and Return to Television
